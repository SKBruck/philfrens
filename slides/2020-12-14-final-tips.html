#### Final Paper Tips ###### PHIL 104

Your Document Should:

  1. Be submitted as a .docx file to Compass
  2. Follow these formatting rules:
    1. 12 point black font (I prefer arial or calibri fonts over times)
    2. 1 inch margins all around the pages
    3. Double spaced
  3. Be within 850 - 1000 words

Make sure that you have turned on AUTO SAVE in Word.

### Make Sure You've Fully Broken Down the Prompt Breaking down a prompt works like this: 1. Read the prompt once **all the way through** 2. Read the prompt a second time, and list out all the individual questions or tasks listed in the prompt. 3. Each individual part of the prompt corresponds to a section of your paper

Your Introduction Should:

  1. A clear thesis statement
  2. A brief map of your argument

You do not need to include background information or summaries in the intro. Just tell me what you're arguing and how your paper will develop.

Your Introduction should not include:

  • Filler phrases. This means phrases like:
    • "Since the dawn of time, mankind has pondered the question of..."
  • Other "catchy" opening sentences, poetry, metaphor, or unfalsifiable claims. These phrases undermine your pursuasiveness.
  • "Context of discovery" references. This means no:
    • Descriptions of the order in which you thought things
    • What you were feeling when you thought them
    • Your current psychological state, or
    • Your speculation about how people might feel about your thoughts

Your Thesis Statement Should:

  1. Be clearly indicated by starting with "I argue..."
  2. Include what you are arguing (e.g., I argue that [Author] has the more plausible theory because...")
  3. Include your main philosophical reason for your argument
### Road Map Don't forget to tell your readers exactly where your paper is going. First you're going to talk about [this topic]. Then [another topic]. etc.

The Body of Your Paper Should:

  • Contain a brief summary of Cohen and Brennan's arguments
  • Contain a concise description of at least one way that these two arguments contrast (e.g., Cohen uses ideal theory while Brennan believes he's using non-ideal theory, etc.)
  • Explicate the argument you set forth in your thesis statement
### Objections to Your Argument You are expected to respectfully entertain at least one **strong** objection to **your own** argument. That means you should briefly describe an issue someone might have with your argument, and then show how their concern does not actuall pose a threat to your argument.
### Conclusion In your conclusion, simply wrap up the paper with a short summary of what you did. This should be the shortest part of your paper.
### Works Cited There is no need to use outside resources for this paper. However, you should expect to cite direct quotations and paraphrased ideas from both Cohen and Brennan in this paper. If you're not sure what to cite, [please review the plagiarism guide provided by the UIUC Library](https://guides.library.illinois.edu/citingsources/plagarism). SafeAssign will pick up plagiarism of your own written works in this class. Please be aware that copying and pasting from previous assignments constitutes plagiarism. If you want to use something from a previous work, just cite yourself. For example: "In writing assignment 14, I pointed out [x fact] that I think is applicable here."
### DO's of Your Final Paper: * Double check that you have answered all of the questions in the prompt * Include an explicitly stated thesis statement * Cite parenthetically for direct quotes **and** paraphrased content * Include a Works Cited page or Bibliography (use whatever citation style you're most familiar with) * Check your brevity, spelling and grammar
### DON'Ts of Your Final Paper: * Don't hesitate to email me with questions * Don't hesitate to ask Shelley for an extension if you need one * Don't hesitate to send me outlines to review before the deadline
